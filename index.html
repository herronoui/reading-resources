Books Worth Reading

Curated books for robotics, controls, aerospace, and the math behind them. Click any cover or title to learn more.

Robotics

Modern Robotics

Kevin Lynch & Frank Park

The go-to text for robot kinematics, dynamics, and motion planning. Uses modern matrix notation and comes with free video lectures, exercises, and a Python library.

Read free online

Introduction to Robotics

John J. Craig

The classic first robotics book. Covers homogeneous transforms, inverse kinematics, Jacobians, trajectory planning, and manipulator dynamics in a clear, engineering-first style.

View on Amazon

Probabilistic Robotics

Sebastian Thrun, Wolfram Burgard & Dieter Fox

The bible for SLAM, localization, and Bayesian state estimation in robots. Essential if you work on autonomous navigation, sensor fusion, or anything under uncertainty.

MIT Press

Planning Algorithms

Steven M. LaValle

Comprehensive coverage of motion planning: sampling-based methods (RRT, PRM), combinatorial planning, and decision theory. The full book is free online and widely used in research labs.

Read free online

Controls

Feedback Control of Dynamic Systems

Gene Franklin, J. David Powell & Abbas Emami-Naeini

The standard undergraduate controls textbook. PID design, root locus, frequency response, state-space methods, and digital control. If you take one controls class, it's probably with this book.

View on Amazon

Robot Modeling and Control

Mark W. Spong, Seth Hutchinson & M. Vidyasagar

Bridges rigid-body dynamics and control for manipulators. Covers forward/inverse dynamics, computed torque control, and adaptive control. Great follow-up after Craig or Modern Robotics.

View on Amazon

Optimal Control and Estimation

Robert F. Stengel

Covers LQR, Kalman filtering, robust control, and stochastic optimal control in one place. Directly relevant to AA 212-style coursework and aerospace GNC applications.

View on Amazon

Aerospace

Fundamentals of Astrodynamics

Roger R. Bate, Donald D. Mueller & Jerry E. White

Short, dense, and legendary. Two-body orbits, orbital transfers, rendezvous, and interplanetary trajectories. The standard intro to orbital mechanics used at Stanford and MIT.

View on Amazon

Aircraft Control and Simulation

Brian L. Stevens, Frank L. Lewis & Eric N. Johnson

Flight dynamics, stability derivatives, autopilot design, and simulation. Connects classical control theory to real aircraft and UAV systems. Used heavily in aero programs.

View on Amazon

Math & Foundations

Linear Algebra Done Right

Sheldon Axler

Builds linear algebra from vector spaces and linear maps instead of determinants first. Clean proofs, great for understanding the math behind robotics Jacobians, SVD, and state-space control.

Author's site

Introduction to Linear Algebra

Gilbert Strang

The most intuitive linear algebra book out there. Matrix factorizations, least squares, eigenvalues, all with geometric intuition. Pair with Strang's MIT OCW lectures for free.

Read free online

Convex Optimization

Stephen Boyd & Lieven Vandenberghe

The foundation for modern trajectory optimization, MPC, and learning. Used across Stanford robotics and controls research. Full PDF and lecture videos are free.

Read free online
